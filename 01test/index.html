Digital citizenship

What is digital citizenship?

Digital citizenship is the ability to navigate digital environments safely, responsibly, and respectfully

What is Digital Law?

Digital law refers to the legal rights and restrictions governing how technology is used. It establishes a framework of electronic responsibility for one's online actions, distinguishing between legal (ethical) and illegal (unethical) behavior.

What is AUP?

An Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) is a set of rules or "digital guardrails" created by an organization—such as a school, business, or internet service provider—that outlines how its computer network, devices, and internet resources should be used.

What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism (often misspelled as pleigrism) is the act of using someone else's words, ideas, or creative work without giving them proper credit and presenting it as your own.

It is considered a form of intellectual fraud or cheating. In the context of digital citizenship, it violates the principles of Digital Etiquette and Digital Law.

What is acidimic dishonisty?

Academic dishonesty refers to any act of deception, fraud, or misrepresentation used to gain an unfair academic advantage.

In 2026, the rules for using AI-generated content in academic settings are centered on transparency, human accountability, and instructor discretion.
