Declarations of Independence

what is says

The Declaration of Independence (1776) announces thatthe thirteen American colonies regarded themselves as independent sovereign states, no longer under British rule. It asserts that "all men are created equal" with unalienable rights to "Life, Liberty and the pursuit of Happiness," and that governments derive power from the "consent of the governed".

why we are upset

In the mid-18th century, the relationship between the American colonies and Great Britain soured due to a series of economic and political conflicts following the French and Indian War. The British government began imposing new policies to pay off massive war debts and exert more direct control, which the colonists viewed as an attack on their long-held liberties.

  1. Taxes were being forced on them they were forced to pay high taxes, such as the Stamp Act and Tea Act, without having any say
  2. They lost their self-government the King dissolved their local legislatures and cancelled their charters, meaning they could no longer make their own laws or govern themselves.
